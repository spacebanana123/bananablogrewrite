# River of Dreams
review_score 7
River of Dreams by Billy Joel is one of the less popular Billy Joel albums. To be honest, I do kind of get why. I think there are less bangers of singles that could truly make an album generationally popular like some of Billy Joel's other albums. 
I guess, I should be clear that I don't think this album is undeserving of praise or is unpopular. It's still a good album that services as solid music in my mind. I wouldn't mind a song or two from this album appearing in a long car ride or something similar, but I don't think I would actively seek out any of the songs. 
Generally speaking, I think I like much of Billy Joel's music. I think the songs are good and they touch on emotional beats that I feel do connect with me, but this album didn't really do it. There is a tangent about We Didn't Start the Fire and generational divides here (plus how that connects to Billy Joel's other discography), but I don't really have the time to write that all out. 
Overall, I gave this album a 7 out of 10. It was alright. None of the songs made it into my main album, but I might think about making a roadtrip playlist.
