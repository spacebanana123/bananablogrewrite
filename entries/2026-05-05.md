# Hamilton
review_score 7
Hamilton by Lin-Manuel Miranda is in some sense an album in that it is literally a collection of songs. On the other hand it is a musical that directly intends to have the songs match with actors on a stage. 
Either way this is a series that I'm doing, so I don't really care if this is album enough for anyone else. I have an interesting conundrum with regards to listening to Hamilton from the perspective of an album. It has a series of deeply compelling and catchy songs, as clearly evidenced by its popularity, but also has a kind of rhythm to it that clearly intends to be for a musical. Of course that isn't a fault of the songs, its instead a testament to Lin-Manuel Miranda's writing, but it posits an interesting quesiton for how we deal with the songs. I really like both Cabinet Battle #1 and #2, but they both end off with transitions that feel frustrating without listening to the next song. That is, undoubtedly, part of the point, but makes listening to the songs harder. 
I think the song that suffers from this the least and is an amazing song, both in the musical and on its own, is The Room Where it Happens. I find the overall the vibe of this song to be stellar. It made my main playlist. I think I give this a 7 out of 10 as songs, even if I really like the musical.
