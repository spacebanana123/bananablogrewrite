# Mr. Morale & The Big Steppers
review_score 8
Mr. Morale & The Big Steppers by Kendrick Lamar has very similar problems and appeals to me as <a href="2026-05-13"> Alligator Bites Never Heal</a>. I think, at least for who I am right now, these problems will continue in the future.
1. I can't understand many of the experiences that Kendrick Lamar refers to personally. I've never had to experience the events of fatherhood or grief that define many of the deeply self reflective songs in this album. Not having to experience those events is undoubtedly a positive thing, but also makes me fundamentally less able to empathize.  
2. I do really find the album and songs compelling. This isn't a problem per say and is much more a testament to the quality of writing that Kendrick Lamar has in his songs, but it means I find myself liking the songs because of their catchiness and rhythmic qualities while feeling frustrated that I can't quite understand the songs.
Overall, I have no doubt that as I grow as a person and experience more parts of life, I will gain more experience to understand more of what Kendrick Lamar is talking about, but for me as a person right now, this doesn't make it into my main playlist and is a solid 8 out of 10.
