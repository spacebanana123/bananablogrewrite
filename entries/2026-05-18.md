# U-Turn
review_score 7.5
U-Turn by Kaleb Cohen is a nifty little album that fully deserves its contemporary pop label. In many ways, if I imagine the quintessential being of a contemporary pop album, I would be imagining U-Turn. I feel the essence of pop in my veins. 

On the other hand, this has some unfortunate consequences for my perception of the album. It feels like it has a very similar texture to many of the other songs that I think is very pop like. If you imagine the spectrum between "too similar" and "too different," I think it is trivial to end up on one of the ends of the spectrum, but thus causing me to dislike the songs. It is very tricky problem to disentangle yourself from and I don't envy the position of anyone that has to deal with it. 

Overall, I think I would rate this album a solid 7.5 out of 10. It's good and I liked Boston the best and added it to my main playlist, but I think I might be approaching the point where pop songs are a little tired.
